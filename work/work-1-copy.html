Vookmark

How do we design a simple experience for people to bookmark videos on the web across sites, and watch them later on any device?

Overview

Vookmark is a cross-platform app that allows users to bookmark videos across popular streaming platforms and watch them later on a device of their choice.

My Contributions

Worked on the product design from scratch.
Designed workflows for Web, iOS, Android and Chrome & Safari browser extensions, and established the ground work to unify the experience across these platforms.
Mentored 2 team members (Jr UX Designer, Front-end developer)

Product Designer, Lead
NFN Labs, Full Time
Jan 2017 - Sep 2018

Vookmark was built in-house by NFN Labs. I was apart of the team right from day 1, when we were dabbling with the idea of building this product in the first place. As the lead UX designer for Vookmark, I was responsible for the full design effort, including the website, mobile app, internal dashboards, blog, social media, and the pitch deck.

This article is a brief overview of how we went about building Vookmark.

How we got here

The internet is full of fascinating videos, most of which you really don’t have time for or shouldn’t be looking at during your workday. Most of us save such content as to our browsers (as favourites), email them to ourselves or keep way too many tabs open. And then we just forget about them altogether. We don’t get back to them.

Is there a way to save videos across sites and platforms, you want to watch eventually but can’t spend time right now, in one place?
We had a decent idea of the what the solution,

Save videos across platforms within a click and then watch them later when you have time. simple right?

Can we build a product to solve this?

This question came up during an all-hands session on a Monday morning. We did a quick pulse check and the team was pretty quite confident. We got a rough ball-park estimate from our seasoned in-house dev team.

Wait…We know we can build this app, but should we?

Just because we can, doesn’t mean we should. To build or not to build? To validate this question we went around getting the perspective of friends and colleagues in our office. We were quick to realise that our potential target audience weren’t very different from people around us and our immediate circle of friends. We wanted to dig into the information in and around us. After a few informal talks across teams, we realised that we were addressing an actual user need, that many of us within the office faced.

A lot of the people we interviewed said at different points during the day, they were watching videos on Youtube, Facebook, Vimeo and even Reddit. And there was always at least one video they really wanted to watch but couldn’t , during the course of the day. And then they never got back to it altogether.

Initial Research

After talking to 20+ individuals, we put our findings together, to extract common themes,

  • Are we addressing an actual user problem?

    Yes (90% of the people we spoke to agreed this a problem they faced).

  • Would users really be interested in using such an app?

    Yes (75% of the people were interested)

  • Insight #1

    A majority of them had already created workarounds, but they didn’t stick to it because the workarounds were cumbersome.

    People were adding interesting videos they came across to reading lists on their browser, or marking the link as a favourite. YouTube users were saving content to "Watch later" list.

  • Insight #2

    People often tend to forget the platform on which they saved videos to watch later. . It's only after a fair amount of searching, can users resume watching videos.

    When users wanted to get back to videos they had bookmarked earlier, they were often unsure about the platform they discovered it on (say Facebook, Youtube etc). People had visit their "save for later" on each platform individually to resume watching videos.

  • Insight #3

    People gradually discover interesting videos during their everyday lives.

    While users discovered interesting videos primarily on popular streaming platforms like YouTube, Facebook and Dailymotion, their behaviour was gradual and passive. There wasn’t a definite pattern or time in the day when people came across interesting content, which made it difficult to translate it into actionable insights.

  • Insight #4

    The type of content users watched on each platform varied. The amount of time they spent varied as well.

    The type of content users watched on each platform varied. The amount of time they spent varied as well. We learnt that having a simple way to categorise and organise videos would be a key issue to tackle.

So, What should the app do?

We created rough ideas stemming from our insights in an ideation session. Through storyboarding and speed dating, we hoped to uncover thoughts and behaviors related to these solutions that hadn’t been unearthed in our research phase. We mapped out our most well-received ideas onto a value-effort matrix.

How should the user bookmark videos across platforms and across devices to watch later?

Through testing a range of medium-fidelity prototypes, we started understanding what the ideal process to bookmark videos should be.

Bookmarking videos on the web

On the web, the user would first have to download the browser extension to start saving videos. After the extension is installed, the user can save videos by clicking on the Vookmark button or icon next to the video title.

Bookmarking videos on mobile/iPad

On mobile, the user would be able to save videos by tapping on the Vookmark icon on the share sheet.

Reflections

  • A mobile first approach - After our initial release we had users asking us on why we didn't have a web app. In retrospect, not having the web app as part of our initial release was a mistake. We went in with a mobile-first approach. This meant that while users would be able to bookmark videos on both the web and mobile, they would have to switch back to their phones or tablets to watch the videos.
  • Ability to bookmark on right click - Users wanted to have the option to bookmark videos on right-click. Luckily for us our web app was only a week away from its release and a minor tweak was enough to allow users to save videos on right-click.

Key Takeaways

  • Vookmark was featured on the Chrome Webstore within a week of its release
  • Looking back, Vookmark was a huge learning for me. The sheer amount of knowledge I gained within a few months was humbling.
  • Building a product from scratch, and going through the complete design process  -  gathering requirements, wire-framing, iterating and prototyping relentlessly, learning from user feedback - and watching my designs come to life was a special experience.
  • There were a lot of firsts for me  -  from wearing the hat of a design lead to designing content for social media to seeing how design decisions impact business.
