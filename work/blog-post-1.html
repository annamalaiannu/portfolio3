Lessons I've learnt while leading the product design team

01

Invest time in your report

This seemingly simple step goes a really long way in building a healthy relationship with your reports. Taking time out and focusing on your report - what he needs to grow, what motivates him, what his long term career goals are - helps send the message that you are there for your reports.

02

Aim to care and assist, not manage.

Caring for your report doesn’t mean defending them when there is critical feedback. What caring does mean, however, is doing your best to help your report be successful and fulfilled in her work. Another nuance of respect is that it must be unconditional because it’s about the person as a whole rather than what she does for you.

03

When you have a new recruit, curate situations for small wins.

In cricket, as a batsman, you are always keen to make your first run and get off the mark, regardless of how many years of experience you have under your belt. I’ve seen that this applies in the work environment as well, especially when you have a new member on the team. Having a small quick win goes a long way in calming the nerves and helping the new member feel in place.

04

Self Awareness + Team Awareness

What works for someone doesn’t work for some else. That’s precisely why doling out how you’d approach a problem isn’t neccessarily a great idea. Just like how each cricketer comes with their own approach, dynamic and strategies, each member on your team is likely to have a different recipe for success, and don’t be surprised if its different from yours. You’ve got to recognise and appreciate that each member on the team is different. Embrace diversity as that’s what brings flavour to your team.

05

Talent alone doesn’t suffice

Be mindful of this the next time you come across a fancy portfolio/resume.There are abundant stories in sport, where brilliant talent catches the world by storm, and then fades away. While a premier league team has the luxury of looking for a mid-season transfer or  finding an immediate replacement in the next season, the story in the tech world is completely different. Hiring can be an extremely costly mistake, not just in terms on money but time as well. In worst cases, a talented person without the right attitude can bring down the morale of the entire team.
